# VPN Practical — ProtonVPN

---

## Objective
To understand the working of a Virtual Private Network (VPN) by installing, configuring, and analyzing ProtonVPN, and to study its role in providing online privacy and secure communication.

---

## Tool Used
- **VPN Used:** ProtonVPN (Free Tier)  
- **Official Website:** [https://protonvpn.com](https://protonvpn.com)  
- **Verification Tool:** [https://whatismyipaddress.com](https://whatismyipaddress.com)

---

## Steps Performed

1. **Account Creation**  
   - Visited [protonvpn.com](https://protonvpn.com) and created a free account using an email ID.  
   - Verified the email to activate the account.

2. **Download and Installation**  
   - Downloaded the ProtonVPN client for Windows.  
   - Installed the application and signed in with the registered account.

3. **Connecting to VPN**  
   - Launched ProtonVPN and selected a server (Netherlands).  
   - Clicked on **Connect** to establish the VPN connection.

4. **Verification of Connection**  
   - Opened [https://whatismyipaddress.com](https://whatismyipaddress.com) to check IP address before and after VPN connection.  
   - Noted change in IP address and country.  
   - Captured screenshots for both states.

5. **Speed and Functionality Check**  
   - Compared browsing speed with VPN ON and OFF.  
   - Observed slight speed reduction after enabling VPN.

6. **Analysis and Documentation**  
   - Researched ProtonVPN encryption protocols, privacy features, and limitations.  
   - Compiled findings in `report.md`.

---

## Observations

| State | IP Address Location | VPN Status | Result |
|--------|----------------------|-------------|--------|
| Before Connection | India | Disconnected | Real IP visible |
| After Connection | Netherlands | Connected | IP changed successfully |

**Observation Summary:**  
ProtonVPN successfully masked the real IP and routed traffic through an encrypted tunnel. Browsing was slightly slower but stable.

---

## Key Features Observed

- AES-256 encryption for secure data transfer.  
- No-logs policy ensuring user anonymity.  
- DNS leak protection and kill-switch feature.  
- Option to use WireGuard and OpenVPN protocols.  
- Free version includes unlimited bandwidth.

---

## Limitations of the Free Plan

- Limited servers (Netherlands, Japan, USA only).  
- Slower connection speeds during high traffic.  
- Secure Core and Tor features are available only in paid plans.  
- One active device connection at a time.

---

## Advantages

✅ Strong encryption and privacy protection.  
✅ Trusted Swiss-based company with open-source clients.  
✅ No user activity logs maintained.  
✅ Easy-to-use interface for beginners.  
✅ Unlimited data even in the free plan.

---

## Disadvantages

❌ Limited countries in free version.  
❌ Slight reduction in browsing speed.  
❌ Some streaming services block VPN IPs.  
❌ Advanced features available only for paid users.

## Conclusion
ProtonVPN effectively encrypts internet traffic and hides the user’s real IP address, ensuring secure and private communication.  
It demonstrates how VPNs protect user data on public networks.  
Despite minor limitations in speed and server access, ProtonVPN (Free Tier) is a reliable tool for learning VPN functionality and online privacy.
